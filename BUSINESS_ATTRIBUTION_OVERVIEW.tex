\section{Methodology \& Framework Architecture}
%=============================================================================

\subsection{Framework Design Principles}

The Mega Business Optimizer employs a multi-layered analytical approach combining:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Business Type Taxonomy}: Comprehensive categorization across five strategic archetypes
    \item \textbf{Characteristic Profiling}: Seven-dimensional assessment including margin, growth potential, capital intensity, brand power, viral coefficient, CAC, and churn
    \item \textbf{Strategy Generation}: Five tailored strategies per business type addressing specific optimization opportunities
    \item \textbf{Simulation Engine}: Monte Carlo-style validation with 1000 iterations per strategy
    \item \textbf{Performance Attribution}: Quantitative measurement of success rates and improvement metrics
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Seven-Dimensional Business Assessment}

Each business type is evaluated across seven critical dimensions:

\begin{equation}
\text{Business Profile} = \{M, G, C, B, V, CAC, \chi\}
\end{equation}

Where:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item $M$ = Typical Margin (0-1 scale)
    \item $G$ = Growth Potential (0-1 scale)
    \item $C$ = Capital Intensity (0-1 scale)
    \item $B$ = Brand Power (0-1 scale)
    \item $V$ = Viral Coefficient (0-1 scale)
    \item $CAC$ = Customer Acquisition Cost (categorical)
    \item $\chi$ = Churn Rate (0-1 scale)
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Strategy Optimization Framework}

Each strategy is evaluated using a composite success function:

\begin{equation}
S_{\text{strategy}} = f(M, G, B, V) \times \text{Risk}^{-1} \times \text{Execution}_{\text{quality}}
\end{equation}

Success rates are calculated through iterative simulation:

\begin{equation}
\text{Success Rate} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{1000} \mathbb{1}(S_i > \text{threshold})}{1000}
\end{equation}
